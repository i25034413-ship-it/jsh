Local Cuisine

Savor the authentic flavors and culinary traditions of Huaibei

A Taste of Huaibei

Huaibei's cuisine reflects the rich cultural heritage of the Huai River region, combining northern boldness with southern refinement

Huaibei Sa (Huaibei Paste)

Huaibei Sa is the most iconic culinary specialty of the region, a unique paste-like dish that has been a staple of local cuisine for generations. Made from a carefully balanced mixture of mung bean starch, wheat flour, and various seasonings, this savory dish is known for its smooth texture and rich, umami flavor.

The preparation of authentic Huaibei Sa requires precise temperature control and timing, skills that have been passed down through family kitchens for centuries. It is typically served hot with a drizzle of sesame oil, chili flakes, and fresh scallions, creating a harmonious balance of flavors and textures.

Signature Dish 2 hours to prepare Savory & Rich

Traditional Shaobing (Baked Flatbread)

Huaibei's traditional Shaobing is a crispy, layered flatbread that is baked to golden perfection in clay ovens. This beloved street food comes in both sweet and savory varieties, with fillings ranging from sesame and sugar to scallion and five-spice pork. The secret lies in the meticulous layering technique that creates dozens of thin, flaky layers.

Each Shaobing is handcrafted by experienced bakers who have spent years perfecting their craft. The dough is repeatedly folded and rolled to create the characteristic layered structure, then brushed with egg wash and sprinkled with sesame seeds before baking. The result is a satisfyingly crispy exterior with a soft, aromatic interior.

Street Food Classic Made Fresh Daily Crispy & Aromatic

Lintong Tea Culture

The ancient town of Lintong in Huaibei is renowned throughout China for its distinctive tea culture that dates back several centuries. Unlike the tea traditions of southern China, Lintong's tea culture revolves around unique herbal blends and traditional brewing methods that have been preserved by generations of tea masters.

Visitors to Lintong's historic teahouses can experience the full tea ceremony, from the careful selection of leaves to the precise water temperature and brewing time. The local tea blends often incorporate regional herbs and flowers, creating flavors that are distinctly Huaibei. The teahouses themselves, many of which are centuries old, provide an atmospheric setting for this cherished social tradition.

Cultural Heritage Herbal Blends Since Ming Dynasty

Roasted Mutton Specialties

Influenced by the culinary traditions of Northern China, Huaibei's roasted mutton is a hearty and flavorful dish that showcases the region's love for bold, satisfying flavors. Local chefs marinate premium cuts of mutton in a secret blend of spices for hours before roasting them slowly over charcoal, resulting in tender, juicy meat with a smoky, caramelized exterior.

The dish is typically served with fresh flatbread, pickled vegetables, and a selection of dipping sauces. During the winter months, the aroma of roasting mutton fills the streets of Huaibei's old town, drawing locals and visitors alike to the family-run restaurants that have perfected this art over generations.

Northern Specialty Charcoal Roasted Winter Favorite

More Local Delicacies

Explore the diverse culinary landscape of Huaibei through its most beloved dishes

Huaibei Hand-Pulled Noodles

Thick, chewy noodles served in a rich, aromatic broth with tender beef slices, fresh vegetables, and a variety of condiments. A comforting meal enjoyed by locals at any time of day.

Pan-Fried Dumplings

Crispy-bottomed dumplings filled with seasoned pork and vegetables. These golden parcels are a popular breakfast item and snack, best enjoyed fresh from the pan with a soy-vinegar dipping sauce.

Sweet Sticky Rice Balls

Soft, glutinous rice balls filled with sweet sesame or red bean paste, traditionally enjoyed during festivals and celebrations. Their round shape symbolizes family unity and happiness.

Traditional Braised Dishes

Slow-cooked meats and vegetables in a rich soy-based sauce with star anise, cinnamon, and other aromatic spices. These deeply flavorful dishes are the cornerstone of Huaibei home cooking.

Where to Eat

Try the old town area near Stone Street for the most authentic local food. The Sui-Tang Canal Ancient Town also offers excellent dining options in a beautiful setting.

Food Markets

Visit local morning markets for fresh seasonal ingredients and breakfast specialties. The night markets come alive after sunset with sizzling street food stalls.

Tea Experience

No visit to Huaibei is complete without experiencing the tea culture at Lintong Ancient Town. Plan at least half a day to fully appreciate this tradition.
